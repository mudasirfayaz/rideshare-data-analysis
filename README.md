# Rideshare Data Analysis — SQL + Power BI Project

An end-to-end data analytics project exploring **2.5 years of rideshare operations data** using MySQL for analysis and Power BI for visualization.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes 2.5 years of rideshare operations data to uncover key drivers of revenue, demand patterns, driver performance, rider retention, and payment reliability.

Using SQL for analysis and Power BI for visualization, the project focuses on identifying actionable insights that can improve growth, operational efficiency, and user retention.

---

## 🗃️ Dataset

| Table         | Rows   | Description                                |
| ------------- | ------ | ------------------------------------------ |
| trips         | 20,000 | Core fact table — one row per ride request |
| drivers       | 400    | Driver profiles, vehicles, and ratings     |
| riders        | 1,600  | Rider profiles and lifetime stats          |
| users         | 2,000  | Shared user records for drivers and riders |
| locations     | 40     | Zone definitions with lat/lon and type     |
| payments      | 16,827 | Payment record per completed trip          |
| reviews       | 15,136 | Ratings and comments per trip              |
| cancellations | 2,966  | Cancellation details                       |

**Source:** [Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/rockyt07/uber-sql-database).

---

## ❓ Business Questions

1. How is monthly revenue trending, and how volatile is it?
2. When is demand highest — by hour and day of week?
3. Who are the top-performing drivers, and who is underperforming?
4. Who are our most valuable riders, and are we retaining them?
5. Are payments completing successfully, and which methods dominate?

---

## 💡 Key Findings

- Revenue remains stable with low volatility (CV ~5.41%) but shows no sustained growth trend, indicating a lack of strong growth drivers.
- Demand is highly concentrated during weekday commute hours (morning and evening), reflecting predictable, routine-based usage patterns.
- Driver performance is driven by efficiency and reliability rather than volume; top performers maintain high completion rates and strong revenue per trip, while underperformers are primarily impacted by high cancellation rates.
- A significant portion of revenue is generated by a relatively small group of high-value riders, who also exhibit the strongest retention behavior.
- Low-frequency riders (one-timers and casual users) contribute disproportionately to churn, highlighting weaknesses in early-stage user engagement.
- Payment success rates are consistent across methods, but a large share of transactions and revenue is concentrated in a few dominant methods, making their reliability critical to business operations.

---

## 📈 Business Implications

- The business is stable but requires targeted growth strategies to increase revenue momentum.
- Peak demand periods should be optimized for supply allocation and pricing strategies.
- Driver performance management should focus on reliability and efficiency rather than volume alone.
- Retention efforts should prioritize onboarding and early-stage user engagement.
- Payment system reliability is critical for dominant methods due to their high transaction volume.

---

## 🚀 Recommendations

- Improve onboarding experience to reduce one-time user churn.
- Introduce incentives for high-performing drivers to maintain service quality.
- Optimize pricing and driver availability during peak commute hours.
- Develop targeted retention campaigns for medium-value users.
- Monitor and strengthen reliability of dominant payment methods.
